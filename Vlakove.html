Orient Express La Dolce Vita (Italy)

The Orient Express La Dolce Vita is a luxurious train service set to launch in Italy in 2024, offering a nostalgic yet modern journey through Italy's most
scenic regions. Operated by Accor and inspired by the 1960s era of Italian glamour, "La Dolce Vita," this train promises opulent design, fine dining, and
immersive cultural experiences on routes from the Alps to Sicily.


The Vande Bharat Express

The Vande Bharat Express is a high-speed, semi-high-speed passenger train operated by Indian Railways. Known as India's first indigenous high-speed train,
it was designed and manufactured domestically, featuring modern facilities and a streamlined design for efficient and comfortable travel.
The Vande Bharat Express connects major cities, reducing travel time significantly while providing a more comfortable, premium train experience for passengers.


The Paragon fleet trains

The Paragon fleet is a series of modern intercity trains operated by Hull Trains in the UK. Launched in 2019, these bi-mode trains can run on both electric and diesel power,
offering greater flexibility and efficiency. Designed with passenger comfort in mind, Paragon trains feature spacious seating, free Wi-Fi, power outlets, and improved accessibility.
They primarily serve the route between London King's Cross and Hull, delivering a fast, comfortable, and eco-friendly travel experience.


Big Boy

The Big Boy is a legendary series of massive steam locomotives built in the 1940s by the American Locomotive Company (ALCO) for the Union Pacific Railroad. Designed to haul heavy freight
over steep grades, particularly the Wasatch Mountains between Utah and Wyoming, each Big Boy locomotive is nearly 133 feet long and weighs 1.2 million pounds. With a 4-8-8-4 wheel
configuration (four leading wheels, two sets of eight driving wheels, and four trailing wheels), it was the largest and one of the most powerful steam locomotives ever built.
Only 25 Big Boys were produced, and today, a few are preserved in museums, with one, UP 4014, restored to operational condition in 2019 by Union Pacific, bringing its powerful
presence back to the rails for special excursions.


The Tokyo bullet train(Shinkansen)

The Tokyo Bullet Train, known as the Shinkansen, is Japan's iconic high-speed rail system connecting Tokyo with major cities across Japan. Launched in 1964, the Shinkansen revolutionized
train travel with its exceptional speed, safety, and reliability. Trains on this network can reach speeds up to 320 km/h (200 mph), allowing for rapid and comfortable travel between urban
centers. Known for punctuality, cutting-edge technology, and a sleek, aerodynamic design, the Tokyo Bullet Train has become a symbol of modern transportation and innovation.
